# Compliance and Product Wording Guidelines

## 1. Purpose

This document keeps the project safe, responsible, and aligned with hackathon constraints.

MatchPulse may show odds and market insights, but it must not become a betting execution product or a direct betting recommendation engine.

---

## 2. Allowed Features

Allowed:

- show live scores
- show match events
- show odds data
- show odds movement
- show market reaction
- show scenario probabilities
- show risk level
- show confidence score
- show agent accuracy
- show historical performance
- show educational/explanatory insights
- show replay mode
- show fan engagement features without money

---

## 3. Not Allowed Features

Not allowed:

- direct betting button
- wager placement
- betting operator links
- guaranteed winning claims
- “bet on this” wording
- personalized gambling advice
- real-money prediction game
- staking or settlement of sports outcomes
- odds arbitrage execution
- financial advice language

---

## 4. Safe Wording

Use:

- “Scenario probability”
- “Market movement”
- “Market reaction”
- “Risk level”
- “Confidence score”
- “Data insight”
- “Possible scenario”
- “Volatility increased”
- “Signal detected”
- “The market appears to favor...”

Avoid:

- “Bet now”
- “Guaranteed win”
- “Best bet”
- “Lock of the day”
- “Sure pick”
- “This will win”
- “Place a wager”

---

## 5. Recommended Disclaimer

Use this in footer, dashboard, and docs:

> MatchPulse is an informational sports intelligence tool. It does not provide betting, wagering, financial, or investment advice. Odds and scenario probabilities are analytical outputs and may be wrong. Users are responsible for complying with all applicable laws in their jurisdiction.

---

## 6. Product Positioning

Preferred:

> A real-time sports intelligence platform that turns TxLINE data into match insights, market movement analysis, and scenario probabilities.

Avoid:

> A tool that helps bettors win.

---

## 7. UI Rules

- Do not add betting buttons.
- Do not add bet slips.
- Do not add deposit/withdraw flows.
- Do not add operator referral links.
- Do not rank outputs as “best bet”.
- Always show risk and uncertainty near probabilities.
- Always show that data may be stale/degraded/replay.
